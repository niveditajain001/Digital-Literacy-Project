# Phishing
Phishing is a common cybercrime where attackers trick users into sharing sensitive information such as passwords, OTPs, or bank details by pretending to be a trusted source. It usually happens through fake emails, messages, or websites.

For example, a college student receives an email that appears to be from their bank, asking them to update their account details urgently. The email contains a link that looks legitimate. When the student clicks the link, they are redirected to a fake website that closely resembles the bank’s official site. The student enters their login credentials and OTP, unknowingly giving access to the attacker.

Typically, students and young users are targeted because they frequently use online services but may not verify sources carefully. The consequences can include financial loss, unauthorized transactions, and even identity theft.

This type of cybercrime spreads because attackers create a sense of urgency and fear, forcing users to act quickly without thinking. It highlights the importance of verifying links, checking sender details, and avoiding sharing sensitive information online.
